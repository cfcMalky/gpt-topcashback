FAQ

Common Questions

Is TopCashback a scam?

No. TopCashback is highly reputable and has been operating since 2005. They have over 159,000 Trustpilot reviews and Millions of UK members. They make money through sponsored ads and bonus commissions from retailers—passing the base commission entirely on to you.

Why does cashback take so long to clear?

When you buy an item, TopCashback tracks it immediately (showing as 'Pending'). However, they cannot pay you until the retailer's return period ends (often 30-90 days). If they paid you instantly, people could buy items, get the cashback, and then return the item for a full refund.

What is the £5 Plus Membership fee?

When you join, you are automatically enrolled in the "Plus" membership. This tier gives you higher cashback rates and bigger payout bonuses, but TopCashback deducts £5 a year from your earnings. You are NEVER charged from your bank. If you prefer, you can easily downgrade to the 100% free "Classic" account in your settings at any time.

How do I get the £20 Welcome Bonus?

Sign up using a referral link. The £20 will sit in your account as 'Pending'. To unlock it and make it payable to your bank, you must earn £10 in confirmed, payable cashback through normal shopping on the site. (Note: Takeaway purchases often do not count towards this £10 threshold).

What happens if my cashback doesn't track?

Occasionally, ad-blockers or cookie issues prevent a sale from tracking. If a purchase doesn't show up within 7 days, you can submit a "Missing Cashback Claim" via the TopCashback help section. You provide your receipt/order number, and they will chase the retailer for the money on your behalf.

Do I have to pay tax on cashback?

In the UK, cashback earned on purchases you make is generally viewed by HMRC as a discount on the goods purchased, not as taxable income. Therefore, standard cashback shopping is tax-free.
